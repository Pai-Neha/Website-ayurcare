Home Remedies, Dos and Don'ts for Psoriasis

Home Remedies:

  • Keep your skin moisturized with non-scented, hypoallergenic moisturizers.
  • Take warm, not hot, baths to soothe the skin and add colloidal oatmeal or Epsom salts if desired.
  • Expose your skin to natural sunlight, but avoid sunburn and use sunscreen.
  • Apply over-the-counter corticosteroid creams for short-term relief of inflammation and itching.
  • Use a humidifier to maintain indoor air moisture and prevent dry skin.
  • Wear comfortable, breathable clothing to minimize skin irritation.
  • Avoid scratching to prevent skin damage and infection.
  • Consider topical vitamin D creams as prescribed by a healthcare professional.
  • Consult a dermatologist for professional guidance on psoriasis management.
  • Explore light therapy (phototherapy) under medical supervision for severe psoriasis.

Dos:

  • Consult a healthcare professional or dermatologist for a proper diagnosis and treatment plan.
  • Follow the recommended treatment plan consistently and attend regular check-ups.
  • Maintain a consistent skincare routine to keep your skin moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid excessively hot water and harsh soaps that can exacerbate psoriasis symptoms.
  • Protect your skin from sunburn and UV exposure using sunscreen and appropriate clothing.
  • Opt for comfortable clothing that reduces skin irritation and friction.
  • Be patient with your treatment; psoriasis management may take time and varies from person to person.
  • Monitor your skin's response to treatments and make necessary adjustments under professional guidance.
  • Discuss long-term skin management goals and options with a healthcare provider or dermatologist.
  • Seek support groups or counseling to address the emotional impact of psoriasis.

Don'ts:

  • Don't scratch or pick at psoriasis plaques, as it can worsen symptoms and lead to infection.
  • Don't expose your skin to excessive heat, as it can exacerbate psoriasis symptoms.
  • Don't ignore severe or persistent psoriasis; consult a dermatologist for evaluation and guidance.
  • Don't overuse corticosteroid creams without professional advice, as they can have side effects with prolonged use.
  • Don't use perfumed or harsh skincare products that may irritate the skin.
  • Don't forget the importance of a consistent skincare routine to manage psoriasis effectively.
  • Don't skip follow-up appointments with your healthcare provider or dermatologist.
  • Don't self-diagnose or self-treat psoriasis; seek professional medical evaluation and advice.
  • Don't smoke or consume excessive alcohol, as they can impact overall health and psoriasis symptoms.
  • Don't hesitate to consult a dermatologist for severe or unresponsive psoriasis.

Symptoms and Management

Symptoms:

  • Red, raised, inflamed patches of skin covered with silvery-white scales.
  • Itchy, painful, or burning skin in affected areas.
  • Dry, cracked skin that may bleed.
  • Thickened, pitted, or ridged nails in some cases.
  • Joint pain or swelling associated with psoriatic arthritis in some individuals.

Management:

  • Seek professional medical evaluation and diagnosis by a dermatologist.
  • Understand the type and underlying causes of psoriasis to guide treatment decisions.
  • Follow a comprehensive treatment plan, which may include medications, topical treatments, or phototherapy.
  • Maintain good overall health with a balanced diet, exercise, and stress management techniques.
  • Explore available treatments, such as topical corticosteroids, vitamin D analogs, and biologics, under professional guidance.
  • Participate in support groups or counseling to cope with the emotional impact of psoriasis.
  • Monitor your response to treatments and make necessary adjustments under the guidance of a healthcare provider.
  • Be patient, as psoriasis management may be an ongoing process, and results can vary.
  • Consider lifestyle choices that reduce triggers or exacerbating factors, such as stress and smoking.
  • Focus on self-confidence and self-acceptance as you manage psoriasis and its impact on your life.

Additional Information

Psoriasis is a chronic skin condition that affects millions of people worldwide. Its symptoms can vary in severity, and management often requires a combination of medical treatments and lifestyle adjustments. Seek professional guidance for the best treatment plan for your specific type of psoriasis. Additionally, addressing the emotional impact of psoriasis and connecting with support groups can enhance your overall well-being and quality of life.
